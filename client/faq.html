Why did you found Karamba Security?

As the FBI recently warned, cars are becoming targets for hackers.

With our background in endpoint security, we saw an opportunity to provide the automotive industry a completely hardened solution that could keep drivers safe from cyber attacks.

What problem in the market do you solve?

Karamba addresses the automotive industry's need to protect the safety of connected cars against cyberattacks. In recent months, we have seen the Jeep Cherokee, Toyota Prius, Tesla Model S, Nissan Leaf and other cars successfully attacked by white hat attackers. Exploits ranged from turning on the radio to killing the car's engine as it drove on the freeway! In response to the Jeep hack, Chrysler recalled 1.4 million vehicles. With Karamba, automotive vendors can protect the externally connected devices in their vehicles, locking down the Electronic Control Units (ECUs) to immediately detect attack attempts and keep the car safe from any type of exploit or malware that could compromise its safe operation.

4. How big is this problem?

Analysts estimated that 20% of vehicles sold worldwide in 2015 would include some form of embedded connectivity. Gartner predicts that by 2020, the number of connected cars sold globally will be 250 million. This represents a Compounded Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 45%, which is 10x the CAGR of the traditional automotive industry.

PricewaterhouseCoopers and Booz & Allen predict that by 2020, the safety technologies category within the connected cars market will reach $41 billion in sales, with a CAGR of 28%. The entire internet of things (IoT) security market is predicted to reach $120B by 2020.

How do you solve it?

Karamba has purpose-built a solution that hardens the externally connected controllers within an automobile against hackers. Typically, these controllers manage the telematics (sensors, instrumentation, navigation, etc.), infotainment (radio, head unit, etc.) and on-board diagnostics (OBD) of the vehicle. Karamba ensures only valid code and valid behaviors are allowed to run on the controllers to keep the car safe and prevent the attacks from ever infiltrating the car's Controller Area Network (CAN Bus).

What do you mean by "block hackers at the gate"?

Karamba focuses on hardening the controllers within the car that are open to external access (via the Internet, WiFi, Bluetooth, etc.). These are the controllers that hackers can use to get into the car's CAN Bus and launch their attack, making them, in essence, the gates to the car. Karamba stops the attacks on the controller to ‘block hackers at the gate.' As a result, the attacks never make it inside, never reach the CAN Bus, and never have access to any of the internal ECUs that control the car's functions.

What makes your solution unique vs. other in-car security companies?

Rather than looking for hackers that are already in the car's internal network (a.k.a. CAN Bus), Karamba is focused on detecting and stopping an attack from getting into the CAN Bus in the first place. Karamba's end-point solution has been purpose-built to protect the externally connected controllers of the car. This approach provides early detection of threats targeting the vehicle and complete malware prevention to ensure the ongoing integrity of the car's operations.

What are the benefits of your Controller Hardening approach?

  • Provides an Early Warning of Threats — alerting on any attack attempts (Droppers), so they can be addressed before they materialize.
  • Stops Attacks at the Gate — preventing attacks from ever making it into the car and establishing a foothold that could be used to compromise the function of any of the car's internal ECUs.
  • Generates No False Positives — allowing only the code and applications that have explicit permission to run on the controller. This creates no ambiguity — either the code is part of the factory settings or not.
  • No False Positives — allowing only the code and applications that have explicit permission to run on the controller, creates no ambiguity. Either the code is part of factory setting or not, no false alarms.
  • No Updates — always providing security for the current version of the software in the car. The solution is embedded in the development cycles of the controller. When the controller is updated, Karamba is updated to reflect the new factory settings. Regardless of changes in the threat landscape, the version of the ECU in use in the car is protected.
  • Requires Only Short Quality Checks — embedding Karamba's software into the ECU software, which means only that system (e.g. the telematics controller) needs to be checked to ensure it performs according to its specifications. There is no need to test the entire car.
  • Retrofit Now — enabling car manufacturers to ensure current models are secured. The simple setup and quick quality check means Karamba can be deployed during a regular car maintenance or service offering.

Who are your target customers?

Karamba's solutions are designed to help car companies and tier-1 system providers, who build the car's externally connected controllers, keep drivers safe from hackers.

Car companies don't want to tarnish their reputation, incur the costs of recalls, or lose future sales as the result of their vehicles being compromised. Tier-1 vendors also have their reputation and revenues to uphold; they are held accountable if the car is penetrated via their system. Both need a way to harden the controllers against attacks to ensure they cannot be used to get in and compromise the safety of the cars' drivers and passengers.

Can your approach be integrated with existing car models, on the road?

Yes, the solution can be used to protect both new and existing car models. Automobile manufacturers can retrofit the cars on the road now, as part of the ECU software update, whenever the car comes into the dealer for regular maintenance.
